Introduction to Soulsborne and Soulslike Games

What are Soulsborne and Soulslike Games?

Soulsborne and Soulslike games are known for their challenging gameplay, intricate world-building, and dark fantasy themes. These games are often characterized by their difficulty, complex lore, and atmospheric settings.

Characteristics of Soulsborne and Soulslike Games

  1. Challenging Gameplay: These games are known for their high difficulty level, requiring players to master combat mechanics and strategies.
  2. Intricate World-Building: The games feature rich and detailed worlds with deep lore and storytelling.
  3. Dark Fantasy Themes: The settings often include dark and gothic elements, creating a unique atmosphere.

Differences Between Soulsborne and Soulslike Games

Soulsborne Games Soulslike Games
Similarity Challenging gameplay, dark fantasy themes, intricate world-building Challenging gameplay, dark fantasy themes, intricate world-building
Difference Developed by FromSoftware, includes titles like Dark Souls, Bloodborne, and Elden Ring Inspired by Soulsborne games but developed by various studios, includes titles like Hollow Knight and Black Myth: Wukong

Where to start in the Soulsborne and Soulslike Game Genres

For newcomers, it is recommended to start with games in the Soulsborne genre. Dark Souls 1 is the the best starting point for those new to the genre. Conquering the game will provide a solid foundation for understanding the mechanics and themes of Soulsborne games, making it easier to transition to other titles in the genre and eventually explore Soulslike games. Then I would recommed Dark Souls 3 next, as it is the most accessible and has a lot of quality of life improvements compared to the previous games. After that, you can explore other titles in the Soulsborne like Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice and Elden Ring. If you have a Playstation, Bloodborne is a must-play. For those interested in Soulslike games. Hollow Knight is a great starting point but if you are looking for gameplay similar to the Soulsborne genre, consider trying Black Myth: Wukong or Lies of P.
